Origin Protocol

ORIGINS & CREATION

// AMAZING FANTASY #15 · AUGUST 1962 //

COMICS

Amazing Fantasy #15

Created by Stan Lee and Steve Ditko. Marvel's editor said "people hate spiders nobody will buy this." He was wrong. Spider-Man got his own ongoing series in March 1963.

CONCEPT

Stan Lee's Vision

A teenage superhero who wasn't a sidekick. Peter Parker lived in Queens with Aunt May, worrying about rent and bullies. No mansion, no billions. Genuinely relatable. Radical in 1962.

DESIGN

Steve Ditko's Art

Ditko designed the iconic red-and-blue costume and web pattern. He created Spider-Man's visual language and co-created Doc Ock, Vulture, Electro, Mysterio, and Sandman.

CHARACTER

Peter Parker

Orphaned young, raised in Forest Hills, Queens. Science prodigy and photographer for the Daily Bugle. Bitten by a radioactive spider on a school field trip. One of the most fleshed-out civilian identities in comics.

CORE

Core Principles

Relatable everyday problems. Guilt as permanent motivation. Intelligence as a superpower. Humor as coping. No mentor, no team. Alone and figuring it out. These principles made Spider-Man immortal.

LEGACY

Cultural Impact

30,000+ comic issues. 9 major films. Dozens of animated series. Countless video games. Recognizable in virtually every country on Earth. The most relatable superhero ever created.

Ability Database

POWERS & ABILITIES

// SPIDER-BITE AUGMENTED PHYSIOLOGY //

💪

Superhuman Strength

Can lift 10-25 tons. Stopped a runaway car one-handed. Held up a collapsing building (ASM #33). Trades blows with Rhino, Venom, and cosmic-level threats.

🧱

Wall-Crawling

Clings to any surface via inter-molecular attraction. Not suction, not hooks. Works on wet glass, ceilings, and smooth metal.

🔴

Spider-Sense

Precognitive danger warning. Directional, works while sleeping. Responds to INTENT. Venom famously does not trigger it.

⚡

Superhuman Agility

Can dodge bullets already fired. Changes direction mid-air. Combat maneuvers physically impossible for normal humans.

🕸

Web-Shooters

Self-invented mechanical devices. Tensile-strength webbing for swinging, binding, and shields. Webs dissolve in ~1 hour.

🧠

Genius Intellect

IQ 250+. Expert in biology, chemistry, physics, engineering. Created web-shooters as a teenager. His mind is as much a superpower as his spider-bite.

🔧

Spider-Tracer

Self-designed tracking devices tuned to his spider-sense. Tracks enemies without line-of-sight.

💊

Accelerated Healing

Heals far faster than normal humans. Recovers in hours from injuries that would take weeks. Has survived hits from Hulk-level threats.

ROGUES GALLERY

// THE GREATEST VILLAIN ROSTER IN COMICS //

Green Goblin

Norman Osborn · ASM #14 (1964)

The greatest Spider-Man villain. Discovered Spidey's identity through his own intelligence. Murdered Gwen Stacy and changed comics forever.

Doctor Octopus

Otto Octavius · ASM #3 (1963)

Four mechanical arms fused to his CNS. 24-ft reach, 8 tons each. Swapped minds with Peter and became the Superior Spider-Man for a full year.

Venom

Eddie Brock + Symbiote · ASM #300 (1988)

Alien symbiote bonded with Eddie Brock. Does not trigger spider-sense. Has all of Peter's powers plus alien biology. "We are Venom."

Carnage

Cletus Kasady · ASM #361 (1992)

Symbiote offspring bonded to serial killer Cletus Kasady. More powerful than Venom with zero moral restraint. Spider-Man's most terrifying villain.

Electro

Max Dillon · ASM #9 (1964)

Living electrical battery. Generates and controls electricity. One of Steve Ditko's originals. Featured heavily in The Amazing Spider-Man 2.

Kraven the Hunter

Sergei Kravinoff · ASM #15 (1964)

"Kraven's Last Hunt" (1987) is widely considered the greatest Spider-Man story ever written. He drugs Spidey, buries him alive, wears the costume.

The Vulture

Adrian Toomes · ASM #2 (1963)

Elderly aeronautical engineer with electromagnetic flight harness. One of Spider-Man's oldest enemies. Brilliantly portrayed by Michael Keaton in Homecoming.

Sandman

Flint Marko · ASM #4 (1963)

Can transform his body into sand, nearly impossible to physically harm. Steve Ditko original. Featured in Spider-Man 3.

The Lizard

Dr. Curt Connors · ASM #6 (1963)

Most tragic villain. Peter's mentor who transforms into a primal reptilian creature with no memory of being Connors.

Mysterio

Quentin Beck · ASM #13 (1964)

Master illusionist and special effects expert. Brilliantly played by Jake Gyllenhaal in Spider-Man: Far From Home.

Story Database

LEGENDARY ARCS

// THE COMICS THAT DEFINED A LEGEND //

The Night Gwen Stacy Died — 1973

ASM #121-122. Green Goblin throws Gwen off the George Washington Bridge. Spider-Man's web catches her but the whiplash breaks her neck. The end of the Silver Age of comics.

Kraven's Last Hunt — 1987

Kraven drugs Spider-Man, buries him alive, puts on the costume, and proves he is a better Spider-Man. Widely considered the greatest Spider-Man arc ever written.

Maximum Carnage — 1993

Carnage assembles a team of villains to unleash chaos on NYC. Spider-Man and Venom must team up. A 14-part crossover.

The Clone Saga — 1994–1996

Spider-Man discovers he may be a clone. Ben Reilly (the Scarlet Spider) might be the real Peter Parker. One of the longest storylines in Marvel history.

One More Day — 2007

Aunt May is shot and dying. Peter makes a deal with Mephisto and trades his marriage to Mary Jane to save her. One of the most controversial stories ever.

Superior Spider-Man — 2013–2014

Doctor Octopus swaps minds with Peter, lets Peter die, and becomes Spider-Man. A full year exploring what Peter's powers look like filtered through a villain.

Spider-Verse — 2014–2015

Every Spider-Man from every universe hunted by the Inheritors. The first time every Spider-Man appeared together. Inspired the animated film trilogy.
